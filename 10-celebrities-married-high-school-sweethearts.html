10 Celebrities Who Married Their High School Sweethearts

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, May 21, 2024

Sometimes celebrities find love outside of the limelight and there are also times when they believed that they’ve found their soul mate at a young age. There are celebrities who continue to be happily married with their high school sweetheart, while others ended their relationships in divorce. It makes you wonder whether or not fame had anything to do with the break-up of some of these couples. Let’s check out some of the celebrities who decided to say “I do” to their adolescent love.

(Photo by Dan MacMedan/WireImage)

Jeff Daniels and Kathleen Treado

While Daniels married his non-showbiz love when they were both 24, they actually met while attending high school in Michigan and sparks were flying then. The couple continues to stay strong with three children as the whole family moved back to their hometown where it all started.

(Photo by Rodrigo Vaz/FilmMagic)

Bono and Ali Hewson

Everybody knows that being married to a rock star is quite difficult. Bono could have easily succumbed to the hedonistic lifestyle of rock n’ roll, but his wife Ali keeps him grounded. These two were childhood sweethearts in Ireland and they tied the know in 1982. He married at a young age of 22.

(Photo by Anthony Harvey/Getty Images)

Jon Bon Jovi and Dorothea Hurley

Here’s another reason to revitalize your belief in getting hitched with rock stars. Bon Jovi and Hurley actually dated for four years in high school. The best part is that the musician actually took time off touring to elope with his sweetheart in Las Vegas secretly. They are currently together and proud parents of four kids.

(Photo by Desiree Navarro/WireImage)

Snoop Dogg and Shaunte Taylor

Not all marriages are perfect and smooth sailing. Snoop met his lady love back in high school and they were hitched in 1997. They have three kids together. However, he filed for divorce in 2004 citing irreconcilable differences. The good news is that their divorce didn’t push through and they renewed their vows fours years later.

(Photo by Tiffany Rose/WireImage)

Robin Thicke and Paula Patton

Thicke and Patton actually met at a teen night club in 1993. They dated throughout their time in high school and in college. In 2005, they got married and had their first son five years later. Unfortunately, the two decided to split in February 2014 and many speculate that it had to do with Robin’s antics with Miley Cyrus and female fans posing for a photo.

(Photo by Dan MacMedan/WireImage)

LeBron James and Savannah Brinson

These two aren’t married yet, but they’ve been together since they were in high school. They even have two sons together to keep them company. Marriage is definitely in their trajectory since the athlete popped the question on New Year’s Eve 2011.

(Photo by Christopher Polk/Getty Images for ESPY)

Ron Howard and Cheryl Alley

Howard may have a number of films under his belt, he’s proud to have one lady love for the majority of his life. He and Alley actually had a forbidden romance thing going on and they would often sneak out just to see each other. The couple married in 1975 when both of them were only 21 years old.

(Photo by Stuart Wilson/Getty Images)

Russell Wilson and Ashton Meem

Football star Russell Wilson met Ashton Meem at a high school party and were together ever since. They even managed to stay together when they went to colleges that were really far from one another. Meem did transfer to a closer school later on to be closer to her boyfriend. The couple got married in 2012, but it sadly ended in divorce last month.

(Photo by Frederick M. Brown/Getty Images)

Olivia Wilde and Tao Ruspoli

Before Jason Sudeikis, Wilde got hitched when she was only nineteen years old to Tao Ruspoli on a school bus in Washington, Virginia. The actress admitted that they chose the venue to keep the ceremony a secret and they only had a few witnesses present. They got married in 2003, but the duo eventually sought for divorce in 2011.

(Photo by Kevork Djansezian/Getty Images)

Mitt Romney and Ann Davies

The politician actually met his future wife when they were both in elementary school. However, Mitt only asked Ann out when they were teenagers. Their first date was to see The Sound of Music in theaters. It seemed that he was convinced that Ann was the one when he informally proposed to her during their high school prom. They married four years later in 1969 and have been going strong ever since with a large family of their own.

(Photo by Kevork Djansezian/Getty Images)
